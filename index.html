Layers of Silence: A Digital Story on Patriarchy and Intersectionality

1. Concepts Addressed & The Narrative

This project explores patriarchy (a social system of male dominance) and intersectionality (how inequalities like gender, caste, and class overlap to produce complex discrimination). To illustrate these abstract sociological ideas, the project draws on the powerful real-life case of Bhanwari Devi, a rural social worker from Rajasthan who faced violent retaliation for stopping a child marriage. Her experience highlights how discrimination against women is exponentially worsened when it intersects with rigid caste hierarchies and economic vulnerability.

2. Creative Approach & Format

Moving beyond a conventional essay, this project takes the form of an interactive digital storyboard. The website allows viewers to explore the narrative by scrolling through visually distinct sections. Short narrative moments from Bhanwari Devi's case are paired with explanatory notes connecting the events to broader theoretical concepts. Utilizing minimal graphics, clear typography, and a dynamic timeline layout, the format translates structural inequalities into an accessible, relatable, and engaging human experience for peers.

An Indian Incident

The Catalyst

Rajasthan, 1992

Bhanwari Devi, a grassroots social worker (Sathin) employed by the state government, campaigns against the prevalent practice of child marriage in her village of Bhateri. She successfully prevents the marriage of a one-year-old girl.

Concept: Patriarchy

The Violent Backlash

Her intervention angers the dominant patriarchal structure of the village. For challenging the traditional authority of men and daring to assert her agency, she faces severe social boycott and ultimately, a brutal gang rape orchestrated by dominant-caste men to "teach her a lesson."

Concept: Intersectionality

The Systemic Silence

Bhanwari Devi's struggle for justice reveals a deeper, overlapping prejudice. The police delay her report, and the courts initially acquit the men, citing that upper-caste men would not rape a lower-caste woman for "purity" reasons. Her gender, lower-caste identity, and rural class status intersected to deny her fundamental justice.

Timeline: Gender in India

1829

Abolition of Sati

An early legislative milestone against violent patriarchal practices, though initiated under colonial rule.

1950

The Constitution

Adoption of the Indian Constitution, guaranteeing equality before the law and prohibiting discrimination on grounds of religion, race, caste, sex, or place of birth.

1992

73rd Amendment & Bhanwari Devi

While grassroots democracy reserved seats for women in local panchayats, the Bhanwari Devi case exposed the brutal ground-level resistance to women's empowerment.

1997

Vishaka Guidelines

Sparked directly by Bhanwari Devi's fight, the Supreme Court lays down historic guidelines to prevent sexual harassment at the workplace.
